But there is a problem

But we want to focus on a different aspect of this violence epidemic.

We want to focus on the externalities.

Violence and Toxic Stress


  • Shonkoff et al. (2012) documented lifelong effects from exposure to violence and adverse childhood experiences in early childhood.
  • Shern, Blanch, & Steverman (2014) suggested identifiable externalities to individuals living with toxic stress.
  • Warner (2017) suggested that these effects vary by sex.

The Data

FCPS Data

  • Student-level Daily Attendance Records
  • Student Demographics
    • race, sex, grade, EL status, FRL, Special Education
    • homeless/highly mobile indicator
    • repeated grade indicator
  • School Identifiers
  • Student level demographic covariates: sex, race, FRL, homeless/highly mobile, retained in grade indicator, EL, SWD, & grade level.
  • Student attendance - Absent, Half-Day Attendance, & Full-Day Attendance; Recoded as Full-Day Present/Not-Full Day Present
  • School Name/ID used to include school level fixed effects.

Crime Data

  • Statute Descriptions
  • Start/End Timestamps
  • Address of Crime
  • Used PostGIS (2.3.6) Tiger Geocoder to geocode the address data running PostgreSQL (9.6).
  • Privacy concerns create challenges with accessing and using data.
  • Inconsistencies in address data (e.g., same street address with different zip codes) leads to erroneous geocoding values.
  • Statute descriptions don't always translate to non-law enforcement personnel that easily.

Derived Data

  • Crime Exposure
  • Crime Proximity
  • Attendance Day of Week Indicators
  • Rolling Sum of Crimes
  • Crimes within Geospatial Buffers (e.g., 0.5, 1, & 2mi)
  • Minimum distances within Rolling Sums
  • We assume students would not be exposed to crimes that take place from 0800-1600 Monday-Friday.
  • We used the geodist (Picard, 2010) package in Stata to estimate the distance from students' home addresses to the crime location.

Data Preparation

  • Crime data lag attendance data by 1 day.
  • Statutory descriptions reclassified from 309 to 5 distinct values.
  • Standardized the proximity measure to avoid scale-based artifacts.

Methods

Modeling Challenges

  • Absences are rare.
  • Multiple crimes can affect attendance decision simultaneously.
  • Multiple time dimensions.
  • Computational cost for estimating models with Mixed Effects is prohibitive.
  • Data are too large for the most popular SVM libraries.
  • Spacetime proximity != social proximity.
  • Limited model fit statistics for Mixed Effects GLM.
  • Multiple spatial relationships per day
  • Additional time dimension per day.
  • When all of the school and other effects are parameterized individually, the matrix is something like 10M x 100

Model Fitting

  • Random Forests
  • Complementary Log Log
  • Pooled Logit/Probit
  • Fixed Effects Logit
  • Receiver Operating Curves

Discussion

Important features

  • Proximity to crime seems to be an important feature (e.g., minimum distance to a crime) in predicting student absence.
  • Male students are more likely to be absent than female students.
  • Economically disadvantaged students are more likely to be absent compared with their counterparts.

Unmodeled Variables

  • Long-term cumulative effects
  • Seasonality effects
  • Absence due to illness and/or family emergency
